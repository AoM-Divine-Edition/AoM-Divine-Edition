As the goddess of fertility, Xochiquetzal was believed to have the power to bestow children upon those who worshipped her, and was often invoked by couples who were seeking to conceive. In addition to her role in promoting human fertility, Xochiquetzal was also closely associated with the fertility of the natural world, and was believed to have the power to bless crops and ensure a bountiful harvest. Her association with fertility was also reflected in her depictions in Aztec art, which often featured her holding flowers or other symbols of abundance and growth. Overall, Xochiquetzal's association with fertility was a central aspect of her role in Aztec culture, and her influence was widely felt in both the spiritual and practical realms of Aztec life.

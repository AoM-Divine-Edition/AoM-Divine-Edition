Xochiquetzal was a revered Aztec goddess who was widely associated with beauty, fertility, love, and creativity. She was particularly known for her patronage of crafts, especially those related to textiles, weaving, and embroidery. In Aztec mythology, she was believed to have taught the ancient people of Mexico how to spin cotton and create intricate patterns using natural dyes. As the goddess of beauty, Xochiquetzal was also closely associated with the arts of adornment, such as jewelry-making and featherwork. Her influence on the crafts of the Aztec people was significant, and her image was often depicted in textile designs and other artistic works as a symbol of feminine power and creativity.

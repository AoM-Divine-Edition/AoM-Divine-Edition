Xiuhtecuhtli was the god of fire, day, and heat. He was the lord of volcanoes, the personification of life after death, warmth in cold, light in darkness and food during famine. 
Many of the attributes of Xiuhtecuhtli are found associated with Early Postclassic Toltec warriors. The nahual, or spirit form, of Xiuhtecuhtli is Xiuhcoatl, the Fire Serpent
Stone sculptures of Xiuhtecuhtli were ritually buried as offerings. A sacred fire was always kept burning in the temples of Xiuhtecuhtli.

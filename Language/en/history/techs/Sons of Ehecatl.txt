Ehecatl was a major deity in the Aztec pantheon who was revered as the god of wind, air, and breath. According to Aztec mythology, Ehecatl had four sons, they were known as the Ehecatotontli, each of whom was associated with one of the cardinal directions of the wind. Mictlanpachecatl (North Wind), Cihuatecayotl (West Wind), Tlalocayotl (East Wind), Vitztlampaehecatl (South Wind).
